Introduction In the world of Data Science, we often hear the phrase, "Data is the new oil." However, just like crude oil cannot be used directly in our cars without refining, raw data cannot be used directly for analysis without cleaning. Data cleaning, also known as data cleansing or data scrubbing, is the process of fixing or removing incorrect, corrupted, incorrectly formatted, duplicate, or incomplete data within a dataset. While building fancy machine learning models sounds exciting, data cleaning is actually the most critical step in the entire data science pipeline.

Color Theory:

Use color to highlight data, not just for decoration.

Be mindful of color-blind friendly palettes.

Consistency: Don't switch styles halfway through a dashboard.
